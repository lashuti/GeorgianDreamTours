The Grand Tour
7 nights & 8 days


Embark on an epic journey through the diverse landscapes and rich heritage of Georgia with our "Grand Tour," spanning 7 nights and 8 days. Explore the historic wonders of Tbilisi and Mtskheta before delving into the ancient history of Gori, birthplace of Joseph Stalin. Discover the cultural treasures of Kutaisi, including the majestic Gelati Monastery and Prometheus Cave, before marveling at the natural wonders of Martvili Canyon. Journey to the remote mountain villages of Mestia and Ushguli, UNESCO World Heritage Sites renowned for their ancient stone towers and stunning alpine scenery. Explore the historic city of Zugdidi and its grand palaces before heading to the charming town of Sighnaghi for a taste of Georgian hospitality. Conclude your grand adventure with visits to the renowned wine regions of Kvareli before returning to Tbilisi, having experienced the best of Georgia's history, culture, and natural beauty.

Itinerary

Day 1 - Tbilisi – The city tour

  • Arrival at Tbilisi International Airport. Meet your guide and drive to Tbilisi. Check-in to the hotel at noon.
  • After short rest, start a sightseeing tour in Tbilisi.
    • Start at Metekhi church and the monument to Vakhtang Gorgasali, the legendary founder of Tbilisi.
    • Take cable car to Narikala Fortress to enjoy breathtaking panoramic view of the city.
    • Walk down to the district of Sulphur bath houses passing by Special Mosque in Tbilisi.
    • Stroll through the Sharden area full of narrow streets with cozy cafés, wine tasting bars, souvenir shops, and breathe in the living past.
    • Stop by the most famous contemporary symbol of Tbilisi - The Bridge of Peace.
    • Continue with Sioni Cathedral, Anchiskhati Basilica, and the clock tower of famous Rezo Gabriadze Theatre.
    • Take a walk through famous Rustaveli avenue, visit old flea market of Tbilisi called - The Dry Bridge - place where memories are sold.
  • Overnight in Tbilisi

Day 2 - Tbilisi -> Mtskheta -> Gori -> Kutaisi

  • After breakfast at the hotel, leave Tbilisi and drive towards Gori.
    • On the way, visit Jvari Church, a true architectural masterpiece of the early Medieval Period built on a hilltop.
    • From Jvari, visit Svetitskhoveli cathedral, one of the most famous and important cathedrals in Georgia.
    • Reach Gori and visit the Stalin Museum.
    • Continue to Uplistsikhe and explore the cave town dating back to Hellenistic Period.
    • Enjoy wine-tasting in winery of Uplistsikhe.
    • Drive to Kutaisi, the central city of Western Georgia once called "Aia".
    • The road to Kutaisi will take you over Rikoti pass and through the longest tunnel of Georgia (1,722 m).
  • Overnight in Kutaisi

Day 3 - Kutaisi –> Martvili Canyons -> Mestia

  • In the morning drive to Martvili Canyon from Kutaisi.
  • On the way to Martvili, pass local villages with charming yards typical only to this area.
  • Once at The Martvili Canyons, take a small boat trip inside the Canyon to see the waterfall.
  • Enter the gorge's another side and stroll over the canyon's waters watching it changing its colors.
  • In the afternoon, head to mysterious Svaneti region through Inguri gorge.
  • On the way, have a small stop by Enguri Dam, one of the biggest projects of the Soviet Union.
  • Continue to Svaneti, home to a special ethnic group of people – heroic and courageous Svans.
  • Mestia, one of the crossroads of the Great Silk Road, still has medieval stone houses with watchtowers.
  • Take a walk in inner parts of Mestia and enjoy local atmosphere.

Day 4 - Mestia -> Ushguli -> Mestia

  • After breakfast, have a fascinating drive to Ushguli village, the highest standing settlement in Europe (2200 m).
  • On the way, stop by the famous Love tower and several waterfalls falling on the road directly.
  • In Ushguli, stroll through the old narrow streets, visit Lamaria Church and enjoy picturesque views.
  • Explore the Ushguli architectural complex, a valuable architectural and historical monument listed in the UNESCO World Heritage.
  • Return to Mestia.

Day 5 - Mestia -> Zugdidi -> Kutaisi

  • In the morning, visit the Mestia Ethnographic Museum.
  • At noon, depart to Kutaisi.
  • Stop on the way at Dadiani palace in Zugdidi to learn about Georgian royal family.
  • Arrive in Kutaisi and enjoy free time.

Day 6 - Kutaisi –> Prometheus cave –> Tbilisi

  • In the morning, drive to the Prometheus Cave with numerous stalactites and stalagmites.
  • Continue to Bagrati Cathedral (11th century) and Gelati Academy (12th century) built by King David the Builder.
  • In the afternoon, head to Tbilisi.

Day 7 - Tbilisi –> Sighnaghi –> Kvareli –> Tbilisi

  • After breakfast, depart towards the Kakheti region, the cradle of Georgian winemaking.
  • First stop at Bodbe convent, burial place of St. Nino.
  • Continue to Sighnaghi, the city of love and the Royal Town.
  • Next stop at a unique wine cellar Khareba located in a tunnel carved out of the rock.
  • Wine tour and wine tasting. Dinner at Tsinandali Radisson Collection Hotel is optional.
  • Return to Tbilisi.

Day 8 - Departure

  • Check-out from the hotel at noon. Free time until transfer to Tbilisi International Airport. Departure. End of the tour.
