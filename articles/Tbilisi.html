Tbilisi


  • 10:00 Start of the tour (time can be discussed with the guide)
  • Starting the tour from the Holy Trinity Cathedral commonly known as Sameba Cathedral. Built in 2004, Sameba is the highest church in Georgia, it is 101m tall.
  • Then we move forward to the old city, starting with the most famous areas called Abanotubani (area of the baths). Here we will visit the below landmarks:
  • Abanotubani - the ancient district of Tbilisi, known for its sulfuric baths.
  • Leghvtakhevi waterfall which is 22 meters tall. The name Leghvtakhevi comes from Georgian word "Leghvi" which means fig.
  • Narikala Fortress - you can notice this place from many sides of Tbilisi. Overlooking the Mtkvari River, Narikala offers stunning views on the city. You can enjoy a cable car ride to or from the area of Narikala.
  • Kartlis Deda (the mother of Georgia) - proudly overlooking the city, 20m aluminum woman is the symbol of the city
  • Then we continue to explore the city on the other side of the river:
  • Metekhi - historical neighborhood of Tbilisi, located on the elevated cliff that overlooks the Mtkvari River.
  • Rike Park - one of the newest parks located in the heart of Tbilisi. It leads us to the famous - Bridge of Peace - bow-shaped pedestrian bridge, a steel and glass construction illuminated with numerous LEDs, over the Kura River in downtown Tbilisi.
  • Once we take lots of pictures and enjoy the area of old town, we drive to Mtatsminda Park.
  • Mtatsminda Park is an amusement facility located atop Mount Mtatsminda on 770 meter height overlooking the Georgian capital Tbilisi. It is the highest point in Tbilisi. Located on more than 100 hectares. Park has more than 100 year’s history. Mtatsminda Park is the most popular theme park in Tbilisi.
  • Lunch during this tour can be planned upon wish and preference of the guests
  • Once the program is finished, the guide will leave you in the city or drop back to the hotel
  • Tour will be finished around 16:00
